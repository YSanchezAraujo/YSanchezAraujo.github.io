my computing setup

I enjoy reading about other people's computing setups so much (e.g., on Uses This) that I decided to post my own here.

hardware

I have a 2019 macbook air that I take everywhere. I thought long and hard about going back to linux when I was first considering getting a new laptop, but I couldn't bring myself to downgrade to a lesser trackpad. Fortunately, I can still trade macOS for a linux distro at some point down the road.

software

internet

I use Google Chrome to view the internet, and DuckDuckGo to search it. I've become increasingly concerned with giving Google so much of my data, and will be moving to Firefox as soon as pinch-zoom is stably implemented.

Privacy is a human right. The advertising-based funding funding model that the internet currently operates on is antithetical to this (for various reasons - see Maciej Cegłowski's excellent talk on this). So I try to contribute to this apparatus as little as possible without crippling my digital life. To this end, I've modified my hosts file through this tool in order to block any connections to ad servers. Webpages load more quickly, advertisers don't track me across the web, and bandwidth is saved. I block whatever ads and trackers make it through with µBlock Origin, and I keep Facebook, Amazon, and Google from tracking me across the web through Privacy Badger.

research

Most of the research I do involves digital signal processing of some sort, and I've primarily used MATLAB due to its rich and varied electrophysiology libraries (such as FieldTrip, Chronux, and EEGLAB). However, it is proprietary and slow, and I'm quickly drifting into switching to Julia and settling there forever. It's fast, simple, and packages like PyCall allow me to easily use Python packages whenever I need to.
